When picking out your trucks, there is actually a lot more to concider than you think. The whole thing is really based on pinion so it'll probably take a lot of mixing and matching before you get the best for you for the kind of riding you like.

First you want to find an axel that is as close to the actual with of your board as possibe. This provides the most stable leverage points for turning. But if you get wider axels it will be more stable with less controll and a shorter axel will be visa-versa.

As for truck orientation, reverse king pin orentation is usually easier to handle and more customizeable so go with that. This will give u a lower center of gravity, for more controll.

The angle that the truck is at determines how reponsive the truck is when you lean into a turn. for more stable fast rides go with about a 42 degree baseplate angel. For more carvability, go with a standard 50 degreee baseplate angle.

Lastly you have your cusions for the trucks called "Bushings" The harder and more barrel like the more resistaance the truck will have against your turn. If you weigh more or are going down hill you'll want harder bushes. For lighter people and fast turners get more coned and softer bushes. click here for a more indepth explination.
