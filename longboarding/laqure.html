Once you have cut out your deck next you'll need to, sand, lacquer, and apply grip tape to the board.
First, you should sand down the whole board just to take the roughness out of it and so the square can set better. First with about a 200 grit, then to a 400 grit . Make sure to get all of the edges rounded well to prevent chipping.

Then sketch any designs you want on you board now, before you put on the lacquer. (paintings by Andreas Preis)

Now get some clear lacquer and apply three coats at least to make sure no weather can get into the wood and hurt your board.

Lastly, if you drew on the top of your board, get some clear grip tape from any skate shop and stick it to the top of your board to keep your feet in place while riding. If no design, black grip tape will work. If you want the grip tape to make a design, cut it out of the tape before you sticks it to the deck.
