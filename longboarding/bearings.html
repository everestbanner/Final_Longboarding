After you have decided your trucks, you should decide on the wheels. Like the trucks, these have a lot of personal preferences to them, all depending on what kind of boarding you like to do.
The first step to finding the right wheels are the hight. You'll want to make sure that the wheels will fit on your board and have enough room for turning. The taller the wheel, the smoother the ride and the higher the top speed, how ever it will be harder to get going. For more of a free ride small board wheel go between 60mm-70mm wheels, but for a bigger board go for something 70mm-80mm.

Next thing to consider for your wheels would be the edge of the wheel, or the lip. If the wheel has a sharp edge it will have significantly more grip on turns then if you buy a wheel with a rounded edge. If you want to be able to slide, get a rounded edge.

The width of the wheel is also a huge factor in how much grip you have on the road, the more surface contact to the road, the more grip you have, thinner wheels will be better for sliding.

Next to take into consideration is the softness of the wheel which is called the durometer of the wheel. The higher the number the harder the wheel. Softer wheels are used for longboarding from 70a to about 85a. A softer wheel (from 70a-78a) will grip the road a lot better and produce a smoother ride, but will make it harder to slide, then a harder wheel (80a-86a) is more for tricks and will go faster, also a harder wheel will last longer, but you'll be able to feel the bumps of the road better.

Lastly, you have the set of the wheel, where the middle that holds the barring is on the wheel. Some wheels have the bearing holder in the middle and others on the edges. Center set wheels are nice because the have the most grip and will increase you a life time of the wheels, because you can flip them after they ware down. But side set wheels have a bit more handle for braking and catching grip when sliding. Beginners should always go with side set wheels first. click here for a more indepth exsplination.
