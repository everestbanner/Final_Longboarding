The first step to making a longboard in making the deck. The deck is the big piece of wood that you plant your feet on while riding.
First off you have to decide what you want your deck to look like. This part is really up to you, there are a few standard designs you can go with or you can get creative, the only thing to watch for is that you make sure there is enough room for a good longboarding stance, which is your feet planted about shoulder width apart. And you need to make sure the board is narrow at the ends where you'll put the wheels, so that as you're turning the board doesn't hit you wheels and throw you off your board, but make sure you ends where the wheels aren't too small for your trucks.

Some standard dimensions for your board would be around 8-10 inches wide by 32-36 inches long by 1/2-1 inch thick.

The material you want to use for the deck should always be a plywood, due to flexibility purposes. The most used woods to use are baltic birch and maple. They provide the durability necessary for the ride.

A tip for cutting out your board; get a big enough piece of paper to sketch your whole board, fold it in half, down the middle and sketch half your board along the folded edge, then you can cut out a perfectly symmetrical design when you cut away the excess paper. Unfold the paper and trace the design on the wood you are using for the deck.
